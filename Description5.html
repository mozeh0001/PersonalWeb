Venger

  • One of the lead contributors and pioneers to the project in Game Design.
  • Immerses players in a tense escape from mysterious pursuers within fictional 1990s Soviet-style apartments.
  • Inspired by Perturbator – Venger, blending physical and emotional challenges into extreme survival scenarios.
  • Players grow stronger, develop resilience, and transition from fear and passivity to determination and aggression.
  • Gameplay emphasizes adrenaline, pride, and emotional empowerment as players embody the spirit of “Venger” — never breaking down or giving up.
  • Collaborated with a nine-person team including a director, designers, programmers, artists, and a narrative advisor.
  • Project timeline: in development from September 2024 to May 2025.

Level and Game Design

Level Design Blueprints

Enemy Types & Level Ideas

  • Designed enemy behaviors and level layouts emphasizing light, shadow, and sound to create life-or-death encounters for player experience.
  • Designed labyrinthine, dark environments with high-contrast scenes, where light was a tool for survival or danger. Shadows could conceal threats or offer safety, while sound guided players in locating or misleading enemies, creating an intense, high-stakes balance of awareness and reaction.
  • Created level blueprints that emphasized light, shadow, and sound dynamics.
  • Planned enemy types that interacted uniquely with their environments.
  • Designed enemies as puzzles, requiring players to analyze, process quickly, and react rather than rely solely on shooting skills.

Unity Engineering

SDF SFX

  • Developed a VFX particle system for the Echo enemy, adding animations so particles morph, disappear, and reappear like dust taking form.
  • How I made it: Used VFX Shader Graph with SDF particle deformation to morph particles into the Echo enemy’s shape. Added noise, velocity adjustments, and animated lifetime values to create a fluid dissolve-and-reform effect, enhanced with force influences for seamless transitions. The result is an eerie, spectral enemy that feels unpredictable in dimly lit environments.
